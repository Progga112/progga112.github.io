Farhat Tasnim Progga

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Marquette University, working under the supervision of Dr. Sabirat Rubya. Dr. Rubya is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Marquette and co-leads the Social & Ethical Computing Lab, where her research spans human-computer interaction, mobile health technologies, social computing, and responsible AI. My research focuses on human-centered AI, social computing, and digital health for women’s wellbeing. I explore how large language models (LLMs) and storytelling can support perinatal mental health, especially for marginalized communities.

Previously, I was a Data Engineering Intern at Northwestern Mutual, working on automated evaluation frameworks for LLMs, and a Research Intern at the Parkview Mirro Center for Research and Innovation, focusing on AI in healthcare. I have also served as a Teaching Assistant for courses in data science and machine learning at Marquette University.
