About

I am a Ph.D. candidate (ABD) in Computer Science at Marquette University, advised by Dr. Sabirat Rubya. My research lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Responsible AI, and Digital Health, with a focus on how AI-driven storytelling and conversational agents can enhance perinatal mental health support for women from marginalized backgrounds.

I aim to bridge technological innovation with social impact by designing AI systems that are ethical, transparent, and culturally sensitive. My work has been presented at premier venues such as ACM CHI, CSCW, and GROUP, where I shared insights into women’s experiences and challenges with perinatal mental health technologies. I have been honored with the Arthur J. Schmitt Fellowship and the Dean’s Research Enhancement Fellowship for my contributions to socially responsible computing.

Beyond academia, I have worked as a Data Engineering Intern at Northwestern Mutual, where I built automated evaluation frameworks for large language models, and as a Research Intern at Parkview Health, applying AI methods in healthcare informatics. My goal is to advance human-centered, explainable, and equitable AI systems that make healthcare more accessible and emotionally supportive for underserved communities.

Developing a Web-Based Digital Storybook Platform Using Generative AI (2025)

I am designing and developing an interactive digital storybook platform that leverages generative AI storytelling to create personalized and culturally attuned narratives for women’s mental health. This project combines adaptive storytelling, user-centered design, and ethical AI principles to enhance emotional engagement and accessibility for diverse audiences.

Evaluating AI- vs. Human-Generated Stories for Perinatal Mental Health (2025)

This study compares how mothers perceive AI-generated and human-written postpartum stories. Conducted with 400 participants via MTurk, the findings reveal that AI-generated stories are linguistically polished yet emotionally distant, while human stories are authentic, relatable, and culturally resonant. The research highlights the potential of AI-human co-creation for designing emotionally intelligent digital health tools.

Exploring Women’s Perspectives and Barriers in Adopting Perinatal Mental Health Technologies (2024)

Through in-depth interviews with 15 mothers, this qualitative study explores how women interact with perinatal mental health technologies. The findings highlight challenges related to professional help, social support, misinformation, and privacy, emphasizing the importance of trust, cultural inclusivity, and design transparency in digital health systems.

Investigating the Role of Storytelling in Online Postpartum Depression Communities (2023)

This research examines how storytelling functions as both a therapeutic practice and a design strategy for mothers dealing with postpartum depression. Using mixed-methods analysis of interviews and surveys, I found that narrative sharing fosters empathy, connection, and self-reflection, offering a foundation for narrative-based digital health interventions.

Understanding Online Social Support Dynamics in Postpartum Communities (2023)

My early work analyzed over 10,000 posts and comments from online postpartum depression forums to uncover how mothers seek and offer support. Through mixed-method analysis, I identified recurring patterns of empathy, advice-seeking, and stigma negotiation, revealing how online spaces serve as informal mental health ecosystems.
