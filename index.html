About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Marquette University, working under the supervision of Dr. Sabirat Rubya. Dr. Rubya is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Marquette and co-leads the Social & Ethical Computing Lab.

My research focuses on human-centered AI, social computing, and digital health for women’s wellbeing. I explore how large language models (LLMs) and storytelling can support perinatal mental health, especially for marginalized communities.

Previously, I was a Data Engineering Intern at Northwestern Mutual, working on automated evaluation frameworks for LLMs, and a Research Intern at the Parkview Mirro Center for Research and Innovation. I also serve as a Teaching Assistant for Data Science and Machine Learning courses at Marquette.

Research Interests

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Responsible/Explainable AI, Social Computing, and Digital Health. I design and study AI-driven interventions that are culturally sensitive, transparent, and clinically relevant for perinatal mental health support.

LLMs & Personalized Storytelling for Postpartum Wellbeing

Poster — CSCW 2024
